Observation of Terrain Features

  • Pay attention to steep slopes as their inclination increases the risk of avalanches.

  • Be cautious of treacherous slopes, especially during rapid weather changes, as avalanches are more likely to occur.

Snow Layer Characteristics Assessment

  • Be attentive to the presence of ice fractures in the area, indicating potential crevasses that increase the risk of avalanches.

  • Check for exposed snow-capped trees, suggesting wind-driven snow accumulation, raising the risk.

Evaluation of Meteorological Conditions

  • Understand if strong winds may blow snow onto specific slopes, forming wind-driven snow deposits and increasing the likelihood of local avalanches.

Temperature Variations

  • Consider the impact of temperature changes on snow layer stability, especially in cases where high temperatures lead to partial melting of the snow layer.

Avalanche Forecast Review

  • Regularly check local avalanche forecasts to understand expected meteorological and terrain conditions, aiding in identifying potential avalanche risk areas.
